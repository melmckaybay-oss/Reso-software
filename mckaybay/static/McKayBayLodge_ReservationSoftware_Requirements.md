## 1. Business Overview

McKay Bay Lodge is a year-round fishing resort located in Bamfield, a small fishing village on the west coast of Vancouver Island, BC, Canada. The McKay family has been in Bamfield since 1895, with 7 generations of fishing heritage. The lodge serves primarily fishermen who bring their own boats and use Bamfield as a base for sport fishing, though it also attracts hikers, nature enthusiasts, and those seeking relaxation.

The business operates several revenue streams: accommodation (lodge rooms and cabins), all-inclusive meal packages, fishing and wildlife charters, a tackle and fuel shop, and merchandise sales. The resort includes amenities such as a hot tub, lounge, boat moorage (1 main dock with multiple arms — capacity sufficient for all guests), walk-in freezer with dedicated shelf per room for fish storage, commercial vacuum sealers for fish processing, fresh ice, and Wi-Fi. There is a nearby boat launch but no on-site parking or laundry facilities.

Guests typically arrive by boat pickup from Bamfield East Dock. Arrival methods include boat, road, and float plane.

## 4. Booking & Reservation Rules

### 4.1 Scheduling
- **Fully flexible** — guests can arrive and depart on any day
- **Open all year round**
- No set arrival/departure days

### 4.2 Group Bookings (Critical Requirement)
- **One person can book multiple rooms** under a single reservation
- Example: John Edwards books Lodge Rooms 6, 8, and 10 for 8 people — this is ONE booking, not three separate ones
- The primary contact handles all communication and dietary info for the group
- Individual guest names for each room are NOT required; only total headcount and room assignments
- **This is a key pain point with current software (WebRes Pro)** — the system forces separate bookings per room, which is cumbersome and time-consuming

### 4.3 Mixed Booking Types
- A single group may have members on different rate structures (e.g., some in a self-contained cabin, others in lodge rooms on the meal package)
- The system must handle this within one reservation

### 4.4 Dietary Information
- Collected from the primary booking contact at time of booking
- Needs to accommodate allergies and dietary preferences
- The lodge has a set menu but adjusts for dietary needs

### 4.5 Charter Add-Ons
- Charters are booked as add-ons to an existing accommodation reservation
- Need to track which dates, which boat, and how many guests per charter day
- Max 4 guests per boat, 2 boats available

### 4.6 Information Collected at Booking
The following data is collected from the primary contact **at booking time**: name, phone, email, mailing address, number of guests, room/cabin preference, meal package selection, dietary requirements/allergies, arrival date & time, departure date & time, arrival method (boat/road/float plane), own boat (Y/N), boat details (length, name), number of boats, charter fishing add-on, charter dates, wildlife charter interest, returning guest status, and special requests.

**Collected pre-arrival** (not at booking): fishing license status, "how did you hear about us" (marketing tracking).

## 5. Financial Tracking

### 5.1 Scope
- The reservation software tracks **what is owed and what has been paid**
- **Payment processing is handled by separate software** — no credit card processing or payment gateway integration needed
- The system needs to calculate totals based on:
  - Room type × nights
  - Number of people on meal packages × nights
  - Charter days × boats
  - Extra moorage charges
  - Applicable taxes (GST)

### 5.2 Payment & Cancellation Policy
- **Deposit:** No deposit required — credit card is taken at booking to confirm the reservation
- **Payment due:** Full payment at end of stay (or first night of stay)
- **Future change planned:** May move to a 50% deposit model in a future iteration

**Cancellation Policy:**
- **30+ days before arrival:** Full refund
- **15–29 days before arrival:** 50% refund
- **Less than 15 days before arrival:** Full cost of package (no refund)
- Note: Policy is applied with discretion and evaluated case-by-case for personal circumstances

### 5.3 Access Control for Financial Data
- Financial data (invoices, payments, balances) is **backend-only access**
- Front-of-house and other staff cannot see financial details

### 7.1 Branding

  - Arrival method (driving in / private boat / MV Frances Barkley)

### 7.3 Pre-Arrival Email
- **Timing:** 3 days before arrival
- **Content:**
  - Reminder of arrival date and check-in time
  - **Ask for estimated arrival time** (reply to email)
  - **Ask for dinner seating preference:** Early (5:30 PM) or Late (7:00 PM)
  - Confirm dietary requirements on file; ask if anything has changed
  - Travel directions for all three arrival methods:
    - Boat: pickup from Bamfield East Dock
    - Road: gravel logging road, drive to conditions, watch for logging trucks
    - MV Frances Barkley: confirm schedule with operator
  - What to pack (rain gear, layers, rubber boots, fishing licence, sunscreen, camera)

### 7.4 Arrival Reminders
- **30 days before arrival:** Reservation reminder, opportunity to update dietary requirements
- **15 days before arrival:** Final reminder, last chance for changes

### 7.5 Post-Stay Follow-Up
- **Timing:** 48 hours after departure
- **Content:**
  - Thank-you message
  - Feedback request — reply to email or review on Google / TripAdvisor
  - **Rebooking request section** (priority system for returning guests):
    - **First Choice:** Pre-filled with exact same dates, number of guests, and room/unit from their just-completed stay
    - **Second Choice:** Fields for alternate dates, flexible on dates (Y/N), alternate unit preference, expected guest count
    - Clear note: *"Submitting a date request does not confirm your booking. We will be in touch to confirm availability."*
  - Context note: July and August dates fill approximately one year in advance; returning guests have priority

## 8. Core UI — Visual Calendar / Gantt Chart View

### 8.1 Primary Interface
- **Tab chart / visual calendar** is the most important view (inspired by Cloudbeds, WebRes Pro, Lodge Runner)
- Mimics the look of a physical booking chart — a calendar grid with rooms on the Y-axis and dates on the X-axis
- Reservations displayed as **horizontal bars** spanning their check-in to check-out dates
- Each bar shows the **guest name** (primary contact)
- **Hover over a name** to see a quick summary: group size, rooms booked, meal package status, charter add-ons

### 8.2 At-a-Glance Information
- Easily see **who is arriving and departing** on any given day
- Color coding by status (confirmed, pending, checked-in, checked-out)
- Differentiate between lodge rooms, cabins, and the suite visually

### 8.4 Calendar Navigation (Session 4 Requirement)
- Easy back/forward scrolling through dates (arrow buttons or swipe)
- "Jump to date" field — type any date and jump directly to it
- View toggle: week view / month view
- **Previous year view** — historical bookings must remain visible and browsable; staff need to look back at past years to see who stayed when
- **Year-over-year layout** — ability to view last year's calendar alongside (or quickly toggle to) the current/upcoming year; critical for placing returning guests in July/August when rebuilding the season

### 8.5 Guest Search (Session 4 Requirement)
- Search bar accessible from the main calendar view
- Type a guest name (partial match acceptable) and instantly see all reservations for that guest
- Search results show: stay dates, unit/room, number of guests, charter add-ons, and any notes
- Supports returning guest identification — staff can quickly see a guest's full history

### 8.6 Repeat Guest & Priority Booking System (Session 4 Requirement)
- Flag returning guests in the system
- July and August dates are high-demand — the season books approximately one year in advance
- Returning guests get priority access; the post-stay email pre-fills their previous dates as First Choice
- Staff need to be able to map out the next year's calendar based on returning guest requests before opening to new guests

### 8.7 Charter Boats on Calendar (Session 5 Requirement)
- **MB1 and MB2** — McKay Bay's two permanent charter boats; always visible as rows on the Gantt calendar, same as lodge rooms and cabins
- **Contractor Boat #1, #2, #3** — three additional rows for contracted guides with their own boats; hidden by default, toggled on by staff when contractor boats are in use (sometimes up to 3 extra boats beyond MB1/MB2)
- Charter boat rows sit below the Cabins & Suite section in the calendar, in their own **"CHARTER BOATS"** group
- Bookings on boat rows show which guests are on that boat on which dates, same colour-coded bar system as rooms

### 8.8 Daily Operations View (Session 5 Requirement)
- Accessed via the existing **"Daily"** button at the top of the calendar
- Expanded to show all of the following in one view for any selected date:
  - **Departures** — group names checking out, room/unit, number of guests
  - **Arrivals** — group names checking in, room/unit, number of guests, arrival method, estimated arrival time
  - **Housekeeping** — list of rooms/units that need to be turned over that day
  - **In-House Meal List** — every group currently staying, formatted for the servers to set tables:
    - Example: John Edwards — 4 guests, Sarah Mitchell — 2 guests, Joe Bo — 6 guests
    - **Total meal headcount** shown prominently at the top (e.g. "Total dinner guests tonight: 12")
    - Only shows guests on the meal package (excludes self-contained cabin guests who opted out)

### 8.9 Emails Tab — Manual Templates (Session 5 Requirement)
- New **"Emails"** tab in the main navigation bar alongside Calendar, Daily, and Guests
- Shows all three email templates in the software:
  1. Booking Confirmation
  2. Pre-Arrival (3 days before)
  3. Post-Stay Follow-Up (48 hrs after departure)
- For now: **manual use** — staff select a reservation, open the relevant template, fields auto-fill from the reservation data, staff copy and send via their own email client
- Future phase: fully automated triggers (send automatically on booking, 3 days before arrival, 48 hrs after departure)
- Templates match the designs in `McKayBayLodge_EmailTemplates.docx`

### 8.10 Guest Internal Notes (Session 6 Requirement)
- A **🔒 Internal Notes** section added to every reservation detail panel (when you click a reservation bar)
- Yellow-highlighted text area — clearly labelled "staff only — not sent to guest"
- Staff can type any notes: e.g. "John wants lunch at 1pm Tuesday", "requests extra towels", "prefers water-facing room"
- Save button — flashes green to confirm save
- Notes also appear as a yellow preview line when **hovering** over a reservation bar on the calendar
- Notes are never included in any guest-facing emails

### 8.11 Daily Notes (Session 6 Requirement)

### 8.19 Data Persistence — CRITICAL (Session 7 — Priority for Session 8)
- **Problem identified:** Railway wipes the SQLite database (`mckaybay.db`) every time a new version is deployed — all reservations are lost
- **Root cause:** SQLite file lives inside the app's ephemeral filesystem on Railway
- **Solution:** Move database to Railway's **Persistent Volume** (permanent storage that survives redeploys)
- **This must be fixed before entering real guest data**
- Session 8 starts with this fix
